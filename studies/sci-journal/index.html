摘要 / Abstract

A restrained field sheet for fictional microclimate readings.

This study compares two instrumented plots on the invented White Elm floodplain. A reed-margin site and a gravel-bank site were sampled during one clear field day, with simultaneous logging of air temperature, relative humidity, light exposure, and soil response. The figures report invented values with academic-paper conventions: ruled axes, explicit ticks, visible error, and traceable data notes.

The report is organized as a working sheet rather than a dashboard. Fig. 1 presents a day profile that can be switched between sites. Fig. 2 tests whether higher surface moisture corresponded with cooler canopy-adjacent air.

Fig. 3 summarizes repeated chamber readings by substrate class, while Fig. 4 compresses a transect contour and a late-day sparkline into a small plate. The plotted points, bars, and sparkline markers report values in the field register below.

No source data, place, station, or institutional identity is real. The page is a front-end effect study of monochrome scientific-journal graphics and interactive SVG charting.
