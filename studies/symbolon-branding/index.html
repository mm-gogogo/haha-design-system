Veyra Field

A measured identity system for cultural strategy studios, pairing quiet authority with a single warm signal color.

Geometric monogram built from a 13-unit radial field and a centered diamond axis.

Palette

Ink, bone, signal.

The palette stays deliberately narrow: a deep ink ground, a warm bone surface, and one copper accent reserved for orientation, active states, and proof marks.

Ink Primary ground · #11110F
Use for gallery fields, large negative space, and signature marks.
Bone Reading surface · #F2EADC
Use for type, paper panels, and reserved clear-space diagrams.
Signal Accent · #B96B4F
Use sparingly for coordinates, rules, and attention anchors.

Construction

Clear-space is part of the mark.

The symbol keeps one diamond unit of open air on every side. Cropping, crowding, or placing the mark over busy imagery weakens the field relationship.

Logo construction grid 13u / x = diamond
Application rules 01 / 04

Minimum Size

Use the complete mark at 42px or larger. Below that, use the inner diamond only.

Placement

Anchor the mark to a calm corner or center field. Avoid decorative tiling.

Contrast

Pair ink with bone for primary use. Reserve copper for emphasis, not body copy.

Typography

Editorial scale, technical rhythm.

Serif display settings give the system its institutional voice. Monospace labels act as coordinates, keeping the page precise without becoming mechanical.

Guidelines

A compact operating code.

Every application should feel deliberate, sparse, and well measured. The system works best when space is treated as a material rather than a leftover.

01

Lead with pause.

Give the mark and headline room to establish hierarchy before adding detail.

02

Keep color rare.

Use the copper accent like a proof stamp: visible, specific, and never casual.

03

Use grids openly.

Construction lines, coordinates, and margins can stay visible when they add trust.

04

Write with restraint.

Prefer exact language, active verbs, and statements that can stand alone.
